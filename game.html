What is Vex?

Our Team PUPR Vex Robotics is composed by a diverse group of students from
the Polytechnic University of Puerto Rico. We have students from different
branches of engineering. We are very passionate engineering students that love
robotics and learning something new every day about STEM. Our main goal as
a team is to complete and exceed our expectation for our designs and
programs, acieving a top position or, even better, winning. We invite you to go
through our website to meet our team and more. We want you to be part of
our experience.

Over Under

VEX Robotics Competition Over Under is played on a 12' x 12' square field configured as seen above. Two (2) Alliances - one (1) "red" and one (1) "blue" - composed of two (2) Teams each, compete in matches consisting of a fifteen (15) second Autonomous Period, followed by a one minute and forty-five second (1 :45) Driver Controlled Period.

The object of the game is to attain a higher score than the opposing Alliance by Scoring Triballs in Goals, and by Elevating at the end of the Match.

Scoring System

In the VRC Over Under field, there are sixty (60) Triballs, which are used in the game. The field features two netted Goals located on opposite sides, and it is divided by a 2" PVC Barrier, creating a Red Offensive Zone and a Blue Offensive Zone. Scoring Triballs in a Goal earns a team five (5) points, while placing a Triball in an Offensive Zone is worth two (2) points. Additionally, the field includes Alliance-specific pipes called Elevation Bars, situated on each side of the Barrier. These Elevation Bars are used at the end of the match to elevate robots. The height of each robot off the ground is measured to determine its Elevation Tier, and points are awarded based on each robot's Tier relative to others. The Elevation Tiers start at ground level and extend above the Elevation Bar.

During the Autonomous period, the Alliance that scores more points receives an additional eight (8) bonus points, which are added to the final score at the end of the match. Both Alliances have the opportunity to earn an Autonomous Win Point by completing three assigned tasks, regardless of which Alliance wins the Autonomous Bonus.
